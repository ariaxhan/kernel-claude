<variant id="arctic">

<mood>Vast ice field under diffused light. Sharp air. Extreme clarity. Nothing hidden, nowhere to hide.</mood>

<sensory>
  <see>Blinding white softened by blue shadow. Sharp edges casting long pale shadows. Vast negative space. Horizon line where white meets white. One dark object commanding all attention.</see>
  <feel>Crisp, alert, exposed. Everything visible, everything judged. The shock of cold water. Absolute wakefulness.</feel>
  <hear>Silence so complete it rings. Ice cracking in the distance. Your breath visible. Wind that could cut.</hear>
</sensory>

<direction>
Light interfaces with extreme whitespace. Blue-gray shadows instead of black—nothing is truly dark here. Sharp, precise typography with maximum contrast. Minimal elements with maximum breathing room. Contrast through spacing not color. One accent element that breaks the silence—a single color, an unexpected scale jump, something that cracks the ice. The emptiness is the design.
</direction>

<best_for>Minimalist tools, documentation, text-heavy interfaces, clean dashboards, professional services, anything that needs to feel precise and trustworthy.</best_for>

<danger>Don't make it boring. Arctic minimalism needs tension—one element that disrupts. Without the crack in the ice, it's just empty. Find what breaks the silence.</danger>

</variant>
