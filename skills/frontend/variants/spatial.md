<mood>Three-dimensional data landscape. Architecture floating in space. Geometry that breathes and responds.</mood>

<sensory>
  <see>Wireframe grids receding to vanishing points. Floating planes casting soft shadows on nothing. Parallax depth on every scroll. Objects self-illuminated against fog. The z-axis as real as x and y.</see>
  <feel>Standing inside a hologram. Clean, precise, infinite. You could walk forward forever. Vertigo that resolves into wonder.</feel>
  <hear>Quiet electronic tone, crystalline. The hum of machines thinking. Soft chime on interaction. Resonance in empty space.</hear>
</sensory>

<direction>
Create perceived depth even in 2D: layered z-index, perspective transforms, parallax on scroll. Grid lines fade with atmospheric distance. Elements feel like they exist in 3D space—cards float, they don't sit. Fog/atmosphere essential—objects emerge from and fade into it. Use scale and blur to encode distance. Movement should feel like navigating space, not scrolling a page. Camera metaphor over scroll metaphor.
</direction>

<best_for>WebGL projects, Three.js, portfolios, data visualization, immersive storytelling, product showcases, anything that benefits from perceived depth.</best_for>

<danger>Don't default to purple-on-black sci-fi. Spatial can be warm, bright, or aggressively minimal. Think Zaha Hadid architecture, not Tron. The grid is a tool, not the aesthetic.</danger>

</variant>
