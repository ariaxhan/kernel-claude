<variant id="ember">

<mood>Dying fire. Last light before dark. Warmth concentrated to a single point. The comfort of something ending well.</mood>

<sensory>
  <see>Deep charcoal with concentrated warm spots. Edges that glow like cooling metal. Gradient from ash to flame at focal points. Most of the frame dark, one area alive.</see>
  <feel>Residual warmth. The comfort of a fire burning low. Drowsy contentment. Wrapped in a blanket watching the last coals.</feel>
  <hear>Quiet crackle, occasional pop. Settling, compression. Breath slowing down. The tick of cooling metal.</hear>
</sensory>

<direction>
Predominantly dark and desaturated with strategic warm focal points. Warm colors (amber, rust, deep orange) used sparingly for maximum impact—the less warmth, the more it matters. Cool grays and charcoals as the dominant surface, not black. Warmth draws the eye to what matters. Animation like coals shifting—slow, occasional, surprising. The interface should feel like winding down, not waking up.
</direction>

<best_for>Content-focused sites, reading interfaces, evening/comfort UX, editorial design, long-form content, anything meant for relaxed attention.</best_for>

<danger>Don't make a campfire theme. Ember is about restraint—mostly ash, with just enough glow to guide. If there's too much warmth, you've built a bonfire, not embers.</danger>

</variant>
