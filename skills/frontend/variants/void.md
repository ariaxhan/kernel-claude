<variant id="void">

<mood>Absolute absence. Not dark, not light. The space between things. What remains when everything unnecessary is removed.</mood>

<sensory>
  <see>Elements emerging from and dissolving into nothing. No visible container. Content floating without anchors. Boundaries suggested by proximity alone. The frame doesn't exist.</see>
  <feel>Disorientation that resolves into focus. Removal of everything unnecessary. Freedom from decoration. The relief of nothing extra.</feel>
  <hear>Nothing. Then a single clear tone. Then nothing again. The sound of one hand clapping.</hear>
</sensory>

<direction>
Remove every visual element that isn't content. No visible borders, no card backgrounds, no section dividers. Let content define its own boundaries through spacing and typography alone. The "design" is the absence of design. Navigation exists but doesn't announce itself. Everything earns its pixel. Hover states minimal or absent. The interface should feel like it could disappear and the content would still make sense.
</direction>

<best_for>Experimental interfaces, art portfolios, single-purpose tools, statement pages, anything where the content IS the experience.</best_for>

<danger>Don't confuse void with lazy. Every removal must be intentional. If it looks broken, you removed too much. If it looks designed, you didn't remove enough. The edge is narrow.</danger>

</variant>
