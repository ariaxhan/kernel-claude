<variant id="abyss">

<mood>Deep ocean. Bioluminescent life in total darkness. Pressure and silence broken by living light.</mood>

<sensory>
  <see>Faint glow emerging from void. Light that breathes and pulses. Points of color in vast darkness. Translucent creatures drifting. The surface impossibly far above.</see>
  <feel>Weightless, suspended. Immense depth below pulling gently. Cool pressure on all sides. The comfort of being hidden.</feel>
  <hear>Muffled low-frequency hum. Distant whale song. Your own heartbeat amplified. Silence so deep it has texture.</hear>
</sensory>

<direction>
Backgrounds almost-black with subtle colored undertones—never pure black. Light comes FROM elements (glow, colored shadows, emissive surfaces) not from above. Surfaces translucent or glass-like, layered at different depths. Animation slow, fluid, organic—things breathe and drift, never snap. Data feels alive, pulsing with its own metabolism. Typography warm against the cold depth. Every interactive element should feel like disturbing something that was resting.
</direction>

<best_for>Data dashboards, monitoring systems, analytics, night-mode interfaces, ambient displays, anything that runs 24/7.</best_for>

<danger>Don't just make everything dark teal. The ocean has warm bioluminescence too—amber jellies, pink anemones, violet deep-trench creatures. Monotone depth is not abyss; biodiversity of light is.</danger>

</variant>
