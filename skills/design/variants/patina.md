<variant id="patina">

<mood>Aged material. Time made visible. Beauty through wear. The dignity of things that have been used and loved.</mood>

<sensory>
  <see>Warm earth tones, visible texture on every surface. Slight roughness, imperfect edges. Wear patterns where hands have touched. Light catching raised grain.</see>
  <feel>Touching old leather, worn wood, sun-warmed stone. The smooth spot on a handrail. Comfort of the familiar. Weight of history in hand.</feel>
  <hear>Analog warmth. Vinyl crackle. Pages turning. Clock ticking. The sounds things make when they've settled into themselves.</hear>
</sensory>

<direction>
Textured surfaces over flat ones—noise, grain, subtle pattern. Warm, slightly desaturated palette like sun-faded photographs. Rounded corners with slight irregularity, not perfect radii. Typography that feels hand-set, with optical adjustments. Subtle wear indicators on interactive elements—things look touched. The interface should feel like it exists in physical space, subject to time.
</direction>

<best_for>Craft brands, personal blogs, photography portfolios, analog-feeling digital products, heritage brands, anything where authenticity matters.</best_for>

<danger>Don't make it literally look old or "retro." Patina is modern design that acknowledges time and material. Skeuomorphism is not the goal—truth to materials is.</danger>

</variant>
